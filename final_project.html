FINAL PROJECT | DEASY PAD

IDEATION

For my final project, I decided to make a keypad that is suitable for designers while using design software. It is a good start to understand how the keypad works and I would like to imply it on future product designs.

CASING

First Print

I designed this keypad casing using Fusion 360 form tool.

3D model printed out using Creative Ender printing machine.

Top Casing are printed by applying tree support to support the floating part of the model.

I also drew out the key switches, rotary encoder and joystick holes outline.

Second Print

I make the model smaller in size and added hand palm rest support on the casing surface.

Keycap, Rotary encoder and joystick holes are cut. Some of the keycap holes need to have minor adjustment.

The palm rest support is at a weird position, hence it doesn't feel comfortable when placing hand on top

Third Print

Final model casing printed out

Hardware List

1. Arduino pro micro

2. Assorted hookup wires

3. LED strip - WS 2812B

4. Joy Stick

5. Rotary Encoder - Volume Switch

6. Mirror Board

7. m12 screw nut

8. Key switches

9. Key cap

ELECTRONIC

Rotary Encoder

I connected rotary encoder to arduino pro micro using female and male wire.

Joystick

I connected rotary encoder to arduino pro micro using female and male wire.

LED strip light - Neo Pixel Strip

I cut the Male part and soldered it on the LED strip as shown below

Switches

I soldered pin header to GND to allow all switches connecting to GND.

ASSEMBLE

All elements are placed nicely at designated spot. I tape the led strip light as the wires are too long and shorten the switches wire.

I use m12 nut to connect the top and bottom casing. Lastly, place the keycap on switches as well as joystick and rotary encoder cover.
